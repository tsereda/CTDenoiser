## Where the paper stands
- **Framing:** theorem-first for AAAI. Title "One-Step Is Optimal: Unconditional
  Rectified Flows are Noise2Noise Denoisers, and Multi-Step Integration Provably
  Hurts." 4-table body (benchmark, detectability, flow-vs-reg, ssflow-steps) +
  CV/per-image appendix. 3 figures scripted.
- **Core claim (final, 3-seed):** one-step flow **ties** direct regression at
  matched pairing (r=2: +1.88 vs +1.99); multi-step Euler **monotonically erodes**
  it (+1.88→+0.52 over 1/4/8/20 steps). Decorrelated *pairing* is the contribution;
  best label-free = +1.99 dB. Finite-step departure theorem (Gaussian, closed-form)
  proves it; matched-pairing experiment confirms it.
- **Detectability:** ~4 dB PSNR gains erode low-contrast detectability 6–8% without
  fabricating structure.
- **Reproducible:** canonical exports in `results/`; figure scripts in `scripts/`.

## Two new datasets (recommended pair) — **implemented**
The theorem is **not CT-specific**, so pick datasets that stress *both* axes:

1. **General natural-image denoising** — clean images with synthetic noise, run in
   **two noise regimes**: i.i.d. Gaussian *and* spatially-correlated (blurred)
   noise. **Why:** shows one-step flow = regression and multi-step-hurts *outside
   CT*, elevating the theorem from "a CT result" to a **general denoising result**
   — the single biggest lever for the theorem-first AAAI framing.
   *Shipped:* `NaturalImageDenoisingDataset` (`--natural`), noisy/clean and
   noisy/noisy pairing (`--pair-mode`), `--noise-mode {gaussian,correlated}`.
   Reads a clean-image dir (`--natural-root`) or a procedural set (self-contained,
   no download). Training/inference path unchanged.
2. **A second independent LDCT source** — instead of external DICOM (not fetchable
   in-repo), a **low-dose simulator**: the low arm is manufactured from full-dose
   CT with a correlated, signal-dependent noise model. **Why:** proves the clinical
   + detectability result isn't tied to one real acquisition — the "only one
   dataset" robustness a medical reviewer expects.
   *Shipped:* `SimulatedLowDoseCTDataset` (`--sim-ldct`), reusing a
   `convert_dicom_to_h5.py` cache's full-dose volumes (`--sim-source`) or a
   procedural phantom set. Noise model in `ctdenoiser/data/noise.py`.

Both live in `ctdenoiser/data/{dataset,noise}.py`, are wired into `train.py`'s
`build_loaders`, and are covered by `tests/test_synthetic_datasets.py`.

**Rationale:** #1 generalizes the *theory* (best for AAAI); #2 generalizes the
*application* (best for TMI/WACV and rebuttals). Together they cover the two
"does it hold beyond your setting?" questions a reviewer will ask.
